# Assignment 01 — A Conversation Between Brain and AI

**Course:** ITAI 4374  
**Date:** January 26, 2026

---

## What This Was

This assignment was a creative dialogue between a human brain and an AI system. They compare how they learn, recognize patterns, store memory, use energy, and make decisions — and talk honestly about where each one falls short.

---

## Key Points From the Dialogue

**Learning** — The brain can recognize a face after seeing it once or twice and relies on intuition and context. AI needs thousands or millions of examples but stores information more precisely.

**Memory** — The brain is flexible but unreliable — it forgets things and changes memories over time. AI stores information exactly as given, but can't form memories the way people do.

**Pattern recognition** — AI can scan thousands of medical images in seconds. The brain is slower but can understand patterns even when information is incomplete or emotional.

**Energy** — The brain runs on about 20 watts. Data centers running large AI systems consume enormous amounts of electricity. That gap matters more as AI scales.

**Decision making** — AI is fast and consistent. The brain brings feelings, morals, and social understanding to decisions — which slows things down but makes choices more human.

**The conclusion** — They're strongest when they work together. That framing stuck with me because it's not about one replacing the other — it's about what each one is actually good at.

---

## What I Learned

Writing this as a dialogue forced me to think about both sides at the same time instead of just listing facts. The energy comparison was the most surprising part — I knew AI used a lot of power but hadn't thought about how dramatic the gap is compared to the brain.

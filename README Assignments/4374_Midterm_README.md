## What This Was

Our group audited ChatGPT by testing it against three neuroscience-inspired criteria: memory, emotion, and attention. The goal was not to prove ChatGPT is a brain — it's clearly not — but to see where it behaves in brain-like ways and where it falls short.

---

## Experiments

**Experiment 1 — Memory (Chisom)**

Gave ChatGPT three personal facts, then shifted the conversation to unrelated topics, then asked it to recall the original facts.

Result: ChatGPT remembered all three accurately. It can track context within a session. But this is not biological memory — there's no hippocampus, no emotional encoding, no long-term consolidation. If the conversation gets too long or cluttered, details get lost.

**Experiment 2 — Emotion and Sarcasm (Oscar)**

Gave ChatGPT statements that sounded positive but were clearly sarcastic (e.g., "Oh great, another long assignment. That's exactly what I wanted today.") and asked it to interpret the emotional tone.

Result: ChatGPT correctly identified the sarcasm and the underlying frustration. But it doesn't actually feel anything — it recognizes emotional patterns in text because it was trained on them, not because it experiences emotion.

**Experiment 3 — Selective Attention (Judith)**

Created prompts with both a clear instruction and competing distracting information. Asked ChatGPT to follow the main instruction while ignoring the noise.

Result: In one test, ChatGPT failed and responded with "Paris" — a word from a distracting sentence rather than the actual answer. This shows that the system doesn't truly "ignore" distractions. It processes everything and picks the most likely response based on patterns, which sometimes means the distraction wins.

---

## What We Concluded

ChatGPT can approximate some brain-like behaviors in language, but the mechanism is completely different. It doesn't form memories, feel emotions, or consciously focus attention. It produces outputs that look like those things because it learned from human-generated text.

For our final project we planned to expand the audit with more complex scenarios covering more neuroscience modules.
